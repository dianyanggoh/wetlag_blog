Pick A Card

Oct 18, 2015   #psychology  #board games 

“Tip: When running a social deduction game, don’t deal the role cards out. Instead of dealing the role cards out, have players ‘pick a card’… There’s some weird psychological phenomenon wherein players can be grumpy about what they’re dealt, but if they pick the cards, their attitude is better.”

“There’s a psychology topic called Locus of Control. When you feel an internal Locus of Control, like choosing a card, you tend to be happier with the results, while an external Locus of Control, being given a card, makes you feel worse.”

Source: /r/boardgames

It’s a simple tweak that doesn’t change the underlying mechanic. Yet it significantly improves the player experience.
