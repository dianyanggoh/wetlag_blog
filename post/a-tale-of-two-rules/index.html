a Tale of Two Rules

May 28, 2016   #rules 

I’ve been reading “Kobold Guide To Board Game Design”, which contains a collection of game design articles. In “Design Intuitively”, Rob Daviau talks about this rule in the game Tigris & Euphrates, in which you earn four different colored cubes throughout the game.

Your final score is your number of cubes in the color you have the least of.

Contrast this with:

Your final score equals the number of complete color sets you have.

They are exactly the same rule!

But the phrasing not only makes latter much easier to understand, but I think it affects how players think about the game.

It’s like a weird magic trick.
