Beacon and Clairvoyant Bullets

Sep 25, 2015   #game_notes  #game_idea 

I was searching around for this Ludum Dare “Beacon” game to play, but unfortunately it doesn’t seem to be available online anymore. I then somehow ended up playing this other Ludum Dare “Beacon” game instead, and this is the one I’m writing about here.

Around halfway through the game, you’re introduced to these turrets which shoot bullets at you. Except, they’re not shooting directly at you, but rather where they predict you’ll end up. To avoid getting hit, you would have to change your behavior after a bullet had been fired. (e.g. Move or jump if you’d been standing still, change direction or stop or jump if you’d been moving, etc)

To be clear, they’re not homing bullets. Merely clairvoyant.

It’s a mechanic that’s easy enough to understand, but its consequences are a little more nuanced. There’s this subtle moment after “dodging a bullet” and watching it sail past when you acknowledge that hmmmm, yes, that would have hit me. It reminds me of the Oracle Billiards prototype that Jonathan Blow made.

I’d like to try something by stringing together a few of these turrents, all of which would be firing in quick succession. As a player you would then have to constantly override your own decisions. I suspect that would feel rather frustrating, but at the same time I think it’d be this weird, interesting sort of frustration..?
