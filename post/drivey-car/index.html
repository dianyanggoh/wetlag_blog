Drivey Car

Feb 26, 2016   #one-button  #emotion 

I quite enjoyed this one-button, driving game in which you could only steer left.

The gameplay consists of driving round the same simple track until you crashed. Personally, it captured that “frustration aesthetic” of Flappy Bird in a way that Crossy Road never did.

Yellow coins, the in-game currency, spawn as you go around the track. There are also red coins, which make you go faster and leave flaming tracks.

I encountered my first blue coin shortly after reaching a score of 10. I was not expecting it at all when my car started swerving wildy, and I promptly wrecked my car.

WTF.

But it was such a defining moment between the game and me, because it became this personal story. I may have played Flappy Bird for a much longer time, but no stories ever came out of it because all the pipes were the same and there was no specific point around which a story could form.

I think these simple coins have a great deal of merit as a game mechanic.

The red coins primed players to expect interesting effects from picking up colored coins. And the great thing was, these red coins didn’t feel punishing. Going faster made the game harder, but not by that much. Besides, going fast feels good, and flaming tracks are cool.

In that sense red coins were really a gentle introduction to their more brutal blue cousins. But even though the swerving felt random and wild initially, I eventually developed an intuition for driving under their influence.

Throughout the game, these coins carved out the peaks and troughs of my excitment level. Here are some examples of my inner thoughts while playing the game:

Just cruising along
“I’m doing GREAT!”

Coin spawned
“Okay, I’d better be careful not to pick that up.”

Accidentally picked up coin
”“Oh shit oh shit.”

Coin effect wore off
“Phew! I’m doing GREAT!”

I love that these coins never felt unfair, because it’s always my fault when I couldn’t avoid them. They’re a great way of providing progressively harder challenges to overcome, and they make players wonder what’s gonna come next.

I made it to 30 rounds, but I crashed before any new colored coin spawned.

And I wonder.
