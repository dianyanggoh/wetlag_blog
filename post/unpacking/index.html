Unpacking

Apr 2, 2016  

Video: Failure Workshop

In this talk, Adam Saltsman talks about unpacking as the ability for a player to understand what were the events that led up to his failure. He mentions that some games unpack naturally, while for others you just can’t seem to engineer this property.

Is a game that’s hard to unpack necessarily a worse game?

When I think about a game like Threes or Drop7, it can be hard to pinpoint exactly which was the move that contributed to my eventual demise. To me that hints at a level of non-apparent depth waiting to be uncovered, and it makes me inclined to invest more time in a game.

As with a lot of things, maybe it’s helpful to think of “unpackability” as a scale along a slider rather than a binary thing.
